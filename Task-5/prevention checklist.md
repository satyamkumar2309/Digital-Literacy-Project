🛡️ Stay Safe Online: The College Student’s Checklist
1.	Verify Before You PIN (UPI Safety): Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Remember: a PIN is only required to send money or check your balance. If someone sends you a "QR code" or "Collect Request" to pay you, it is a scam.
2.	Enable SIM Platform Lock: Set a PIN on your SIM card. In India, "SIM Swapping" is a common way for hackers to intercept your OTPs. A SIM lock prevents your number from being used in another device if your phone is stolen.
3.	Audit Your "Official" Calls: If you receive a call from someone claiming to be from the Police, CBI, or Customs (often called "Digital Arrest" scams), hang up. Real government agencies never demand money or "clearance" via video calls or WhatsApp.
4.	Use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Enable 2FA on your WhatsApp, Instagram, and University portals. Use an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) instead of SMS OTPs whenever possible to prevent interception.
5.	Beware of "Job" or "Task" Scams: Ignore WhatsApp/Telegram messages offering high pay for simple tasks like "liking YouTube videos." These are precursors to investment fraud.
6.	Secure Your Social Footprint: Set your Instagram and Facebook profiles to "Private." Scammers use public photos to create "deepfake" content or impersonate you to ask your friends for urgent money.
7.	Public Wi-Fi Hygiene: Avoid logging into banking apps or student portals while on free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. If necessary, use a trusted VPN to encrypt your connection.
8.	Scrutinize Shortened URLs: Before clicking on links in SMS (like fake "Electricity Bill" or "Bank KYC" updates), use a URL expander tool to see the real destination. Official Indian bank domains always end in .com or .in—never random strings of numbers.
